About Jenny Saville

Artist Bio

Jenny Saville (British, b.1970) is best known for her rich, naturalistic paintings of large, fleshy women, often featuring images of her own body in her work. Saville was born in Cambridge, England, and attended the Glasgow School of Art in Scotland. An exhibition at the Saatchi Gallery in London, as well as her inclusion in the Young British Artists group, launched her at a young age to international fame in the 1990s. Her style has been compared to that of Lucian Freud and Sir Peter Paul Ruben

Artist Style

Saville's artistic style is a captivating exploration of the human body, characterized by bold brushwork, unconventional proportions, and emotional depth. Through thick, dynamic brushstrokes, Saville creates textured and visceral representations of flesh that challenge traditional notions of beauty. Her figures often exhibit exaggerated features and distorted forms, inviting viewers to contemplate the diversity and complexity of the human form. Beyond mere representation, Saville's paintings evoke a range of emotions, capturing moments of vulnerability, resilience, and strength. With her unflinching portrayal of the body, Saville pushes the boundaries of figurative art, inspiring audiences to reconsider their perceptions of beauty, identity, and the human condition.

Artist's Inspiration

Jenny's inspiration springs from a deep well of humanity's nuances and contradictions. Delving into the rich tapestry of human experience, she finds fascination in the interplay between physicality and emotion. From the mundane to the profound, Saville draws from the raw essence of everyday life, infusing her work with a gritty authenticity. Her canvases become a stage where the complexities of identity and societal norms are laid bare. Through her bold brushstrokes and unconventional compositions, she challenges viewers to confront their preconceptions about beauty and the human form. Saville's art acts as a catalyst for introspection, urging us to embrace the full spectrum of human existence with all its flaws and imperfections.
